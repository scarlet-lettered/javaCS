Logical
Operator Meaning Example
&& "Logical And" (both-and): Both sides of the operation must be true (1 < 3) && (3 > 2)
|| "Logical Or" (either-or): At least one side of the operation must be true (1 > 3) || (3 > 2)
! "Logical Not": Reverses the logical state of the operation, making a true statement false and vice versa. !(1 > 3)
